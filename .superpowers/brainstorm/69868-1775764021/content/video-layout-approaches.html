A — Three-Card Grid

All 3 videos visible at once. Each card has thumbnail, play button, title, and duration. Bridge CTA at bottom leads to calculator. Visitors pick which demo interests them most.

Pros

  • All videos discoverable at once
  • Visitors self-select by interest
  • Scannable — no interaction needed to see options

Cons

  • 3 thumbnails may feel overwhelming on mobile
  • No guided viewing order

B — Tabbed Single Player

One large video player with 3 tabs above it. Clicking a tab switches the video. Focused viewing — only one video visible at a time. More cinematic feel.

Pros

  • Focused attention on one video at a time
  • Larger player = more engaging
  • Works great on mobile (no grid)

Cons

  • Other videos hidden behind tabs
  • Extra click to discover all content

C — Features Replacement Only

No new section. Replace static screenshots in the existing Features section with clickable video embeds. Minimal change to page structure.

Pros

  • Least disruption to page flow
  • No extra page length
  • Videos appear next to their feature descriptions

Cons

  • Videos buried after Calculator — most visitors won't reach them
  • No "proof" moment before the calculator
  • Least impact on conversions

💡 My Recommendation: A + C combined

Use the Three-Card Grid (A) as a new section between PainPoints and Calculator for the "proof" moment. Also replace static images in Features with video embeds (C) for reinforcement. This maximizes exposure: videos appear twice — first as discovery, then as context alongside feature descriptions.
